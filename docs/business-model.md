# Business model — TON

Independent public-sector market-entry compliance for the Kingdom of
Tonga.

- Public procurement is routed through general public-finance
  legislation, not a free-standing Public Procurement Act -- Public
  Finance Management Act 2002, s.44, via the Public Procurement
  Regulations 2015 (amended 22 February 2016 under the Government Act
  (Cap.3), s.9); own text sets institutional thresholds ($7,500 /
  $10,000 / $100,000) for Central Procurement Unit and Government
  Procurement Committee involvement
- Ministry of Commerce, Consumer, Trade, Innovation and Labour
  (MCCTIL), through its online Business Entities Registry -- company/
  business registration (Companies Act 1995, Business Licences Act
  2002, Registration of Business Names Act 2013) **and**, under a
  distinct legal basis (Foreign Investment Act 2020), foreign
  investment registration -- MCCTIL's own genuinely dual role, on one
  integrated platform (businessregistries.gov.to)
- Ministry of Revenue and Customs Tax Identification Number (TIN)
  registration (Form 1 individuals / Form 2 sole traders and small
  business / Form 3 non-individual entities) and Consumption Tax Act
  2003 (15% consumption tax, confirmed via the Ministry's own
  Consumption Tax Overview page)
- Foreign Investment Act 2020 Reserved/Restricted-List gate -- bars a
  filing that declares itself seeking a Foreign Investment
  Registration Certificate (FIRC) for a Reserved-List activity outright
  (11 activities reserved exclusively for domestic investors), and for
  a Restricted-List activity unless that activity's OWN itemized
  Tongan-equity-percentage/paid-up-capital conditions are independently
  satisfied (3 itemized activities, heterogeneous conditions -- not a
  single uniform threshold)
